Web Stuff

I've done a lot.

I've been building websites since the 1990s. It started in college, with a short class at the library on using the World Wide Web and HTTP. From there, I moved on to designing sites with Dreamweaver, to getting curious about hand-coding in the 2000s, and teaching myself HTML, CSS, Actionscript in Flash, and then Javascript.

Recently I completed the Meta Front-End Developer Certification, learning React and more of the latest web frameworks and libraries like Bootstrap and Tailwind. Being a web developer means constantly learning and evolving along with the one constant in life: change.

However, I love to keep things simple and efficient, and I generally will write my own CSS like I did for this site.

Are you a developer that wants to collaborate or delegate some of your own work? I'm available for freelancing. Contact me at valerie@valeriepower.com. Or, Check out my Github.

Photo

I fell in love with photography in junior college. This was in the days of manual cameras and film, darkrooms and developer. Ahh, the smell of the stop bath! I still remember the magic of seeing my first images appear in the dim red light.

I went on to university and majored in photography, earning a bachelor's degree. I then worked in a professional photo lab, where I learned the arcane art of retouching negatives and prints. Meanwhile, I was building my own darkroom in the garage of my apartment.

When I got a job as a photo editor at an educational institution that wrote and produced its own course materials, I gained valuable experience.From shooting corporate portraits and school events, to traveling to Asia and Australia to photograph pearl farms, it was an experience unlike any other.

Lately I haven't done as much photography. I chalk it up to the advent of iPhone photography. I miss the days of looking through the viewfinder instead of squinting at a screen. Installation

I wish I'd known how much I love engineering things when I was younger. I might've majored in that in college.

I love building and making things with my hands. I'm also good at fixing things and figuring out how they work. So, when I got involved with Burning Man, I wanted to make things. Big things!

My second year at Burning Man, I participated in a community art project. It had Center Camp placement. A giant eyeball that people could sit inside and communicate with another node on video, that was across the street at our camp, Furgloo. This was 2005.

I always wanted to learn welding. I did, and ended up using that to make a welded burning angel that we took to the playa in 2006.

Then, I helped make a bottle wall installation called North County Jesus. A stained glass Jesus missing a face, so you could stick your face in the hole. The name was partly a tribute to Charlie (chasterus) at East Jesus. Who, by the way, told me to learn Python back around 2009. I should've listened to him. I still haven't learned Python, but plan to! RIP Charlie.

About Me

I'm Valerie. I've been passionate about the arts my whole life. I wrote a travelog when I was 5, sketched horses and race cars in elementary school, learned drawing and painting in high school, and fell in love with photography in community college. This was in the old days of manual cameras and film, darkrooms and developer. I loved the the technology of it all as much as the art.

At university, I began using the web in its infancy, and using digital imaging programs like Photoshop. I started designing and building websites in the late 90s.

Throughout, I worked at public libraries, a photo lab, and an educational and research institution, gaining valuable experience. I got sent halfway around the world to take pictures. I learned how to write scripting languages, which led me to learn coding in more depth. Along the way, I explored Burning Man, began performing with a backyard circus, and eventually ran a live theater. I recently wrote three suspense novels and we are reviving the circus.

I'm available to build your website, take your photo, and more.
